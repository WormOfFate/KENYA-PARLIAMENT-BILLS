# **The County Governance and Executive Separation Act, 2026**

**A Bill for an Act of Parliament to restructure County-level executive authority into distinct functions of law enforcement, civil administration, revenue management, and oversight, consistent with Articles 176, 179–187, 196–200 and Chapter Eleven of the Constitution of Kenya, 2010.**

**ENACTED by the Parliament of Kenya as follows:**

### Part I – Preliminary
**1. Short title and commencement**  
This Act may be cited as the County Governance and Executive Separation Act, 2026, and shall come into operation upon publication in the Gazette, subject to any necessary constitutional amendments or enabling legislation.

**2. Interpretation**  
In this Act—  
“County Sheriff” means the chief law enforcement officer of the County.  
“Mayor” means the chief civil administrator of the County.  
“County Council” means the deliberative and oversight body comprising Members of the County Assembly (MCAs).  
This Act shall be read subject to the supremacy of the Constitution of Kenya, 2010.

**3. Purpose**  
The purpose of this Act is to—  
(a) Implement a clear separation of executive powers at the County level to enhance accountability, prevent concentration of power, and improve service delivery;  
(b) Establish distinct offices for law enforcement (Sheriff), civil administration (Mayor), revenue collection, and legislative oversight (County Council);  
(c) Align with devolution principles under Chapter Eleven while promoting efficient county governance.

### Part II – Core Separation Principle
**4. Separation of County Executive Functions**  
(1) County executive authority shall be divided into the following distinct functions:  
   (a) County Sheriff – enforcement and coercive authority;  
   (b) Mayor – municipal and county civil administration;  
   (c) County Council – deliberative coordination, legislation, and oversight;  
   (d) County Revenue Commissioner – revenue collection and fiscal administration.  

(2) These functions shall be institutionally separate. No office shall exercise or assume the core powers of another.  
(3) Any conflict of authority shall be resolved by the Judiciary.  
(4) County institutions may declare a state of emergency for notification and escalation purposes only. Such declaration confers no additional powers. All emergency governance authority remains with the National Executive.

### Part III – The Office of the County Sheriff
**5. Election and Term**  
(1) The County Sheriff and the County Attorney (running mate and assistant) shall be elected together for a term of two (2) years, renewable up to a maximum of three (3) terms.  

**6. Qualifications**  
A person qualifies to be County Sheriff or County Attorney if they—  
(a) are a natural-born Kenyan citizen;  
(b) are at least thirty (30) years of age;  
(c) have at least seven (7) consecutive years in law enforcement, military, or emergency response (for Sheriff), or as an advocate/prosecutor (for Attorney);  
(d) have no prior criminal conviction and are not party to any criminal or civil trial at the time of election.

**7. Powers and Responsibilities of the County Sheriff**  
(1) The County Sheriff shall be the primary law enforcement officer and executive head of the County Police Department and emergency services.  
(2) The Sheriff shall have exclusive authority over enforcement of laws, criminal investigations, arrests, execution of warrants, public order, and emergency response services.  
(3) The Sheriff shall have plenary powers to appoint and remove officers.  
(4) The Sheriff and deputies may issue on-the-spot cane lashes (maximum 12 for minors, 39 for adults) or fines for minor offences, subject to the citizen’s right to contest in court.

**8. County Attorney**  
The County Attorney shall prosecute criminal cases, provide legal advice, represent the County in civil matters, and ensure legal compliance with professional independence in prosecutorial decisions.

### Part IV – The County Revenue Commissioner
**9. Appointment and Responsibilities**  
(1) The County Revenue Commissioner shall be appointed by the County Council for a term of four (4) years and may be reappointed. Removal shall be by a two-thirds (⅔) vote of the County Council.  
(2) The Commissioner shall assess, collect, and manage county revenues, maintain records, conduct audits, and advise on fiscal matters.  
(3) The Commissioner shall have no coercive powers; enforcement actions shall be executed exclusively by the County Sheriff upon lawful request.

### Part V – The Office of the Mayor (Civil Administrator)
**10. Appointment and Term**  
(1) The Mayor shall be appointed by the County Governor as the chief civil administrator of the County and shall serve a renewable term of seven (7) years.  
(2) The County Council may impeach the Mayor for financial impropriety, breach of trust, or gross negligence by a two-thirds (⅔) vote.  

**11. Qualifications**  
The Mayor must be a natural-born Kenyan citizen, at least thirty (30) years of age, with at least seven (7) years of relevant professional experience in public administration, finance, or related fields, and with no disqualifying criminal convictions.

**12. Core Role and Exclusive Powers**  
The Mayor shall be responsible for:  
- Development and maintenance of public infrastructure;  
- Oversight of education, healthcare, and social services delivery;  
- Urban planning, local commercial regulation, and municipal civil service;  
- Preparation and implementation of administrative budgets for civil functions.  

The Mayor shall **not** command police, conduct investigations, exercise coercive powers, or control revenue collection.

**13. County Cabinet**  
(1) The Mayor shall be assisted by a County Cabinet consisting of Members of the County Assembly (MCAs).  
(2) County Cabinet Secretaries shall be appointed by the Mayor from among elected MCAs, subject to approval by the County Council (Alderman Board).  
(3) Cabinet Secretaries shall be accountable to the Mayor for operational matters and to relevant national ministries for statewide policy compliance. In case of conflict, the matter shall be referred to the County Council for resolution.  
(4) The same appointment rules shall apply to any additional departments created under State law.

**14. Deputy Mayors**  
The Mayor may appoint Deputy Mayors for different municipalities or sub-counties within the County, subject to approval by the County Council.

**15. Emergency Declaration**  
The Mayor may declare a 72-hour municipal emergency for notification and expedited response purposes only. The County Council must convene within 48 hours to review, extend, or suspend the declaration and approve any emergency budget. No additional powers are conferred.

### Part VI – The County Council
**16. Role**  
The County Council (comprising MCAs) shall serve as the legislative, deliberative, and oversight body. It shall coordinate between the Sheriff, Mayor, and Revenue Commissioner, review ordinances, approve budgets as required, and exercise oversight functions consistent with the Constitution.

### Part VII – Miscellaneous
**17. Transition and Implementation**  
Existing County Governments shall transition to this separated executive structure within thirty-six (36) months. The County Governor shall facilitate the appointment of the Mayor and Cabinet in line with this Act.

**18. Regulations**  
The Cabinet Secretary responsible for devolution, in consultation with the Council of Governors, shall issue regulations for the effective implementation of this Act.

**19. Constitutional Note and Savings**  
This Act strengthens separation of functions within the existing constitutional framework where the County Governor remains the overall chief executive. Provisions on distinct Sheriff and Mayor roles, appointed Mayor by the Governor, and MCA-based Cabinet are designed to operate under Articles 179–187. Any further clarification or enhancement may be pursued through constitutional amendment processes if needed. In case of inconsistency, the Constitution prevails.
