# **The Family and Clan Institutions Recognition Act, 2026**

**A Bill for an Act of Parliament to recognise, establish and operationalise the Sovereign Family Institution and Patriarchal Clan structures as foundational units of society, to promote family stability, generational continuity, pro-natalist outcomes, and internal self-regulation, consistent with cultural values under Article 11 of the Constitution of Kenya, 2010, while proposing certain provisions that may require constitutional amendment or reconciliation with Articles 27, 45, 53 and the Bill of Rights.**

**ENACTED by the Parliament of Kenya as follows:**

### Part I – Preliminary
**1. Short title and commencement**  
This Act may be cited as the Sovereign Family and Clan Institutions Act, 2026 (No. 4 – Revised), and shall come into operation upon publication in the Gazette, subject to any necessary constitutional reconciliation.

**2. Interpretation**  
In this Act—  
“Patriarch” means the male head of the Clan, bearing primary authority and responsibility.  
“Clan” means a multi-generational sovereign institution comprising families linked by blood, marriage or adoption by oath.  
“Sovereign Family Institution” bears the meaning assigned in Section 3.  
This Act shall be read subject to the Constitution; provisions in conflict therewith are presented for parliamentary debate and potential constitutional amendment.

**3. Purpose**  
The purpose of this Act is to—  
(a) Affirm the family as the foundational unit of society — a sacred trust and monarchy under the leadership of the husband and father — for raising godly offspring, stewarding wealth, and transmitting virtue and discipline;  
(b) Establish Patriarchal Clans as sovereign institutions of mutual support, protection, accountability, and self-regulation;  
(c) Promote strong pro-natalist outcomes through stable, multi-generational family structures.

### Part II – The Sovereign Family Institution and Patriarchal Clan Structure
**4. Patriarch and Clan Leadership**  
(1) The Patriarch is the head of the Clan and nominally the father and husband of the founding generation.  
(2) In regions with dispersed relatives, male clan members (not exceeding 50 families) may form a sub-clan and elect a Patriarch.  
(3) The Patriarch, and the Clan by extension, bears responsibility for the welfare and protection of members from conception to natural death, and for enabling their prosperity in life, liberty, and purpose while preserving family harmony.

**5. Marriage**  
(1) Every man has the right to marry a woman of his choosing, provided no paternal or close blood relation within four generations, and following a one-year betrothal. Marriage is a consensual covenant.  
(2) Upon marriage, the husband is the head of the family with primary authority and responsibility for its direction, protection, provision, training, and discipline of children.  
(3) The wife is responsible for the management and care of the household and the training and care of the children.  
(4) Adultery (sleeping with a married woman) is a capital crime, absolved only by the offending man paying a seven-fold dowry to the offended husband with the latter’s voluntary acceptance.  
(5) Infidelity, withholding of conjugal rights, or material deprivation are valid grounds for dissolution of marriage.  
(6) Mothers shall nominate at least two other women as recognised caregivers in case of absence or incapacity.

**6. Composition and Formation of the Clan**  
(1) The Clan consists of blood relatives, those adopted by oath, and those brought in by marriage. It spans four generations to a common ancestor.  
(2) Upon the marriage of the Patriarch, the Clan forms a Sovereign Fund to which all members submit one-fifth (1/5) of their income.  
(3) Clan members contribute skill, time, and effort and benefit from mutual honour, trust, and care.

**7. Clan Governance**  
(1) The Clan is governed by the Patriarch and Clan precedent and culture.  
(2) Clans with twenty or more adult members shall institute a Council of Elders (at least six) to advise the Patriarch and a panel of Deacons to aid execution of his role.  
(3) Positions revert to primogeniture if no heir is designated, but not more than twice in the same generation. The Patriarch prefers the person with highest ability, accepted by the Council of Elders.  
(4) Required Elder positions include Treasury Elder and Records Elder, each aided by up to two Deacons.

**8. Paternity and Parental Authority**  
(1) A man is responsible for children he sires or adopts, and is personally liable for their welfare, training, and discipline. Fathers are held responsible for misbehaviour of minor children.  
(2) Parents have primary and presumptive right and duty to direct the upbringing, education, discipline, and moral formation of minor children.  
(3) A Patriarch may admit members only with two-thirds Elder agreement (except minors adopted by members).  
(4) A Clan may disown a natural-born member by Patriarch initiation and near-unanimous Elder vote. Adopted members may only be dissociated by court process. Formerly disowned natural members may rejoin only after seven years via adoption. Minors under 17 may not be disowned.

**9. Protection of Women and Dowry**  
(1) Women are under the protective authority of their father before marriage, husband during marriage, and eldest son or Clan in widowhood.  
(2) A husband and father shall prepare dowry as security.  
(3) Upon marriage, the woman is presumed adopted into the husband’s Clan, carrying only her father’s dowry and severing prior legal ties.  
(4) In remarriage, children remain in the father’s Clan custody; the new husband may negotiate joint custody but holds primary authority while the biological father lives. A widow carries her husband’s inheritance as dowry.

**10. Inheritance, Family Wealth, and Sovereign Fund**  
(1) Parents may bequeath property to children. No tax on transfers between direct kin.  
(2) Commercial enterprises operating over seven years shall allocate 5–15% of profits to long-serving employees as shared stewardship (equal shares, tradable only within Clan/company for seven years).  
(3) Clans maintain Sovereign Wealth Funds as detailed in the Family and Clan Sovereign Wealth Trusts Act.

**11. Dispute Resolution and Dissolution of Marriage**  
(1) Internal disputes escalate through one Elder, three Elders, then six Elders with Patriarch as tie-breaker.  
(2) Inter-clan disputes use joint Elder councils with neutral tie-breaker.  
(3) Marital dissolution requires exhaustion of arbitration and may proceed to court only thereafter. Joint parental authority continues with presumptive deference to the father.

**12. Independence, Family Duties, and Clan Powers**  
(1) Adult members retain civil liberties but bear sacred duties of care to kin.  
(2) Clans may temporarily deprive members of movement or autonomy (up to one year) by Patriarch decision after Elder hearing, with State notification.  
(3) Clans have legal standing to pursue/defend members’ rights and interest in sexual/reproductive discipline of members.

**13. Limits on State Intervention**  
The State shall not intervene in family or Clan affairs except after exhaustion of internal processes and upon clear judicial finding of criminal harm or gross negligence. Internal governance shall not be redefined by the State except in cases of fraud, physical abuse, or gross negligence, and only to the minimum extent necessary.

**14. Sovereign Family Interest and Jury Trial**  
(1) The family, represented by the husband/father and extended through the Patriarch and Clan, holds sovereign interest in members’ welfare from conception to natural death.  
(2) Family and Clan trials shall primarily use civil jury trial. In gender controversies, juries shall have equal numbers of each gender. In generational disputes, peer advisors may sit with the Jury of Elders.  
(3) Clans may hold members accountable and assist in enforcement of court orders, including detaining members on bond.

**15. Public Office Restrictions**  
(1) No public office is inheritable.  
(2) No Clan shall have more than seven members in the same arm of government, nor more than one-third of offices in any arm, nor more than one-fifth in subsidiary bodies.  
(3) Clan members shall not serve as direct superiors/subordinates except in successive courts.  
(4) Clans shall maintain membership registries with the State.

**16. Extreme Remedies**  
The State Government may suspend Clan leadership, order reorganisation, or dissolve a Clan only upon judicial finding of persistent criminal conduct, systematic abuse, trafficking, or obstruction of justice.

### Part III – Miscellaneous
**17. Transition and Registration**  
Clans shall register with County governments. Existing cultural practices may align within 24 months.

**18. Regulations and Constitutional Note**  
(1) The Cabinet Secretary may issue regulations.  
(2) Parliament notes that certain provisions (e.g., capital punishment for adultery, patriarchal primacy, gender roles in authority, limits on State intervention, disownment powers, and public office quotas) may conflict with Articles 27 (equality), 45 (family as equal partnership), 53 (children’s rights), and other Bill of Rights provisions. These are submitted for full debate, possible constitutional amendment via referendum or parliamentary process, and reconciliation with the 2010 Constitution.

**19. Savings**  
Where parties consent to alternative arrangements, such documented agreements shall prevail, falling back to this Act where conflicts arise.
