# **The Police Service and Law Enforcement Reform Act, 2026**

**A Bill for an Act of Parliament to reform policing by separating State and County functions, improving welfare, introducing on-the-spot enforcement, and creating performance-linked incentives, building upon the County Governance and Executive Separation Act, 2026, and other legislation in this package.**

**ENACTED by the Parliament of Kenya as follows:**

### Part I – Preliminary
**1. Short title and commencement**  
This Act may be cited as the Police Service and Law Enforcement Reform Act, 2026, and shall come into operation upon publication in the Gazette.

**2. Interpretation**  
In this Act—  
“County Sheriff” means the chief law enforcement officer at County level as established under the County Governance and Executive Separation Act.  
“State Police” means the national law enforcement agency responsible for inter-county, transnational, and specialised policing.  
This Act shall be read together with all previous Acts in this legislative series and is subject to the Constitution of Kenya, 2010.

**3. Purpose**  
The purpose of this Act is to—  
(a) Establish clear separation between State Police and County Sheriff functions;  
(b) Improve police welfare and professionalism through better remuneration;  
(c) Empower efficient, on-the-spot enforcement with strong accountability;  
(d) Introduce a transparent 2% fine-sharing mechanism linked to the Sovereign Wealth Fund.

### Part II – Separation of State Police and County Sheriff
**4. Separation of Concerns**  
(1) There shall be a clear functional and operational separation between:  
   (a) **State Police** – responsible for national security, inter-county crimes, terrorism, organised crime, border security, specialised investigations, and support to Counties upon request.  
   (b) **County Sheriff** – responsible for day-to-day law enforcement, public order, minor crimes, traffic, and emergency response within the County (as per the County Governance and Executive Separation Act).  

(2) The State Police shall not routinely exercise County-level policing powers except in declared national emergencies or upon formal request by the County Sheriff.  
(3) The County Sheriff shall not exercise national or inter-county jurisdiction except under coordination with the State Police.  
(4) Coordination mechanisms shall be established through a Joint Law Enforcement Council at national and county levels.

### Part III – Police Welfare and Remuneration
**5. Minimum Salary**  
(1) The minimum basic salary for all uniformed police officers (State Police and County Sheriff departments) shall be raised to **KES 150,000 per month** or its inflation-adjusted equivalent as determined annually by the Salaries and Remuneration Commission.  
(2) This shall be implemented progressively within twenty-four (24) months alongside the fiscal phasing mechanisms in the Direct Citizen Benefit and Public Service Reform Act.  
(3) Additional allowances for risk, housing, and performance shall be prescribed by regulations.

### Part IV – On-the-Spot Enforcement and Demerit System
**6. On-the-Spot Tickets**  
(1) Police officers (State and County) are empowered to issue on-the-spot tickets for minor offences and traffic violations equivalent to **10% of the statutory minimum fine** for the offence.  
(2) The ticket shall be payable by the end of the month or contested in court within fourteen (14) days.  
(3) Each ticket shall attract **demerit points** on the offender’s driving licence or vehicle record:  
   - More than **5 demerits** in a calendar year → **6-month suspension** of licence/vehicle use.  
   - More than **20 demerits** in the same period → **lifetime ban** from driving/vehicle operation (subject to judicial review after five years).  

(4) All tickets and demerits shall be recorded in a transparent national digital register accessible to the public and subject to audit.

### Part V – Fine Collection and Gratuity Distribution
**7. Police Share of Fines**  
(1) The Police department (State or County) that issues a fine shall collect **2%** of the total fine amount as an administrative gratuity.  

(2) **Distribution of Gratuity**:  
   The 2% gratuity shall be distributed equally among:  
   - All members of the relevant department (M), and  
   - All tiers of administration above them (N),  
   with the issuing officer/department receiving **three parts**.  
   Formula: Total gratuity ÷ (M + N + 3).  
   Elected and appointed offices in the chain are included in the distribution.

(3) These gratuities:  
   (a) Shall **not** form part of the officer’s basic compensation package.  
   (b) Shall be taxed as normal income.  
   (c) Shall be remitted at the end of the financial year into the recipients’ **Sovereign Wealth Fund accounts**.  
   (d) Dates, transaction IDs, and amounts shall be published three months prior to remittance for public contest.  
   (e) A **5% service fee** shall be levied on the gratuity and distributed among all other members of the Government Arm (State or County).

**8. Accountability Safeguards**  
(1) Excessive or abusive issuance of tickets shall constitute misconduct and trigger disciplinary action, including dismissal.  
(2) All fines and gratuities shall be subject to retroactive audit under the Taxation and Fiscal Responsibility Act.  
(3) Citizens may contest tickets and demerits in court or before the Alderman Board (arbitration).

### Part VI – Miscellaneous
**9. Transition and Implementation**  
(1) The salary increase and separation of functions shall be implemented within 24 months.  
(2) The on-the-spot ticketing and gratuity system shall be rolled out with digital infrastructure linked to the Sovereign Wealth Fund.  
(3) Savings from improved compliance and reduced court burdens shall support the Direct Citizen Benefit phasing (10%-100%).

**10. Regulations**  
The Cabinet Secretary for Interior and the National Police Service Commission, in consultation with County Governments and the Sovereign Wealth Fund Board, shall issue regulations for the effective implementation of this Act.

**11. Constitutional Note**  
This Act strengthens law enforcement within the framework of devolution and the County Governance and Executive Separation Act. Provisions on salary, gratuity distribution, and demerit systems are designed to improve professionalism and accountability. In case of inconsistency, the Constitution prevails.
