# **The Taxation and Fiscal Responsibility Act, 2026**

**A Bill for an Act of Parliament to give effect to principles of limited taxation, fiscal accountability, pro-natalist reliefs, and strengthened audit mechanisms in public finance, with all tax collections decentralised to County Governments (except at Export Processing Zones and ports of entry), consistent with Articles 201, 203, 209–212, 226–229 and Chapter Twelve of the Constitution of Kenya, 2010.**

**ENACTED by the Parliament of Kenya as follows:**

### Part I – Preliminary
**1. Short title and commencement**  
This Act may be cited as the Taxation and Fiscal Responsibility Act, 2026, and shall come into operation upon publication in the Gazette.

**2. Interpretation**  
In this Act—  
“National Government” means the Government of Kenya.  
“County Government” has the meaning assigned in Article 176 of the Constitution.  
“KRA” means the Kenya Revenue Authority.  
“EPZ” means Export Processing Zones.  
“Clan Trust” and “Family Trust” have the meanings assigned in the Family and Clan Sovereign Wealth Trusts Act, 2026.  

**3. Purpose**  
The purpose of this Act is to—  
(a) Impose strict limits on taxation while decentralising collection to County Governments;  
(b) Promote pro-natalist outcomes through family and Clan reliefs;  
(c) Ensure rigorous KRA audit oversight and retroactive accountability.

### Part II – General Principles and Tax Caps
**4. General Principles**  
(1) All taxation must be authorised by law, publicly disclosed, and administered transparently.  
(2) Taxation shall respect the principles of public finance under Article 201 of the Constitution.

**5. Tax Caps**  
(1) **National Government Taxes** (Income tax and Sales tax):  
   (a) Income tax shall not exceed ten percent (10%) flat rate on net positive financial gain above a statutory minimum.  
   (b) Sales tax (including all surcharges, levies, or regulatory fees) shall not exceed five percent (5%).  

(2) **County Poll Tax**  
Counties may levy a uniform poll tax on adult residents and adult members of Clans headquartered in the County, subject to the strict conditions in Section 6.

(3) All levies, licenses, and fees shall fall within the applicable tax caps.

**6. Remittance Tax, Tariffs and Special Collection Points**  
(1) Remittance tax on outflows by non-citizens and tariffs on foreign goods, labour and services may be imposed without cap.  
(2) **Collection Responsibility**:  
   (a) **All tax collections** shall be undertaken by County Governments, except at Export Processing Zones (EPZs) and ports of entry, where the KRA shall collect directly.  
   (b) KRA shall audit and oversee the entire collection process nationwide.

### Part III – Tax Collection, Remittance and Audit
**7. Collection by Counties**  
(1) County Governments shall serve as the primary collectors of all national and local taxes within their jurisdiction.  
(2) Counties shall remit collections to the National Government Treasury according to a schedule determined by law.  
(3) KRA shall conduct regular and random audits of County collection processes, accounts, and remittances to ensure compliance, accuracy, and prevention of leakage.

**8. Remittance and Revenue Sharing**  
(1) Counties shall remit eighty percent (80%) of collected national taxes to the National Government and retain twenty percent (20%).  
(2) Of the National Government’s share:  
   (a) Not less than ten percent (10%) shall be shared equally among all Counties;  
   (b) Not less than ten percent (10%) shall be apportioned according to population.  
(3) Counties retain full control over locally collected poll taxes and service fees.  
(4) The National Government may, through KRA audits, impose administrative penalties for delayed or inaccurate remittances.

**9. Contestable Tax Bills**  
The National Government (via KRA) and County Governments shall issue contestable tax bills with full supporting accounts annually. Disputes shall be resolved within six months of the following financial year.

### Part IV – Prohibited Taxes and Reliefs
**10. Prohibited Taxes**  
No government shall impose taxes on private property ownership/use, unrealized capital gains, or through statistical/imputed/presumptive means.

**11. Family, Clan and Pro-Natalist Reliefs**  
(1) One percent (1%) deduction per minor child or infirm dependant, up to ten percent (10%) of total tax bill.  
(2) Basic food ingredients and locally produced medical expenses exempt from sales tax.  
(3) Seven-year tax holiday for new enterprises, Families and Clan Trusts (obligations begin in year four).  
(4) Voluntary donations to ministerial kitties attract relief of one percent (1%) per order of magnitude (log₁₀) above the minimum.  
(5) These apply to individuals and registered Clan/Family Trusts.

**12. Payment Flexibility**  
License fees and fines may be paid at 120% within one year or 200% within three years.

### Part V – Budget, Audit and Expenditure Rules
**13. Legislative Appropriation**  
No public funds shall be expended except pursuant to appropriation by law defining clear purpose and measurable targets.

**14. Executive Discretion**  
The Executive may spend less than appropriated amounts or use alternative means without constituting misconduct.

**15. Budget Continuity**  
Budgets operate on a six-month rolling cycle with automatic continuation if no new budget is passed.

**16. Retroactive Audit Principle**  
(1) All expenditures are subject to mandatory retroactive audit.  
(2) KRA shall support audits of revenue collections and remittances.  
(3) Where funds cannot be traced, this constitutes prima facie unlawful expenditure. Burden of proof lies on the responsible authority.

**17. Criminal Liability and Recovery**  
(1) Misappropriation triggers personal liability (including for superiors in cases of negligence).  
(2) Liability is extinguished upon full recovery of funds plus surcharge.  
(3) Performance failure alone is not misconduct.

**18. Performance Incentives and Cleansing Resolution**  
Deliverable-based bonuses and legislative immunity resolutions as previously detailed.

**19. Recordkeeping and Citizen Rights**  
All expenditures and collections must be traceable. Citizens have the right to access lawful use records, subject to confidentiality.

### Part VI – Miscellaneous
**20. Transition**  
All collection systems shall transition to County-led model (with KRA direct collection at EPZs/ports and full audit oversight) within eighteen (18) months.

**21. Regulations**  
The Cabinet Secretary for Finance, in consultation with the Council of Governors, KRA, and Auditor-General, shall issue regulations for seamless County collection, KRA audits, and implementation.

**22. Constitutional Note**  
Provisions on tax caps, County collection primacy, KRA audit role, and retroactive burdens are presented for parliamentary debate and possible constitutional reconciliation with devolution and taxation Articles.

**23. Savings**  
In case of inconsistency, the Constitution prevails unless amended.
