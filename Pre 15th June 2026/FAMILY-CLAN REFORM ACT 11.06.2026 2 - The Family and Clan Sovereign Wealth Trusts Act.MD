# **The Family and Clan Sovereign Wealth Trusts Act, 2026**

**A Bill for an Act of Parliament to establish, operationalise and strengthen Clan and Family Sovereign Wealth Trusts as multi-generational economic pillars under the Sovereign Family Institution, to promote intergenerational wealth stewardship, family stability, pro-natalist outcomes, and Clan self-sufficiency, consistent with Article 11 and Article 40 of the Constitution of Kenya, 2010, while noting potential tensions with certain equality and individual rights provisions.**

**ENACTED by the Parliament of Kenya as follows:**

### Part I – Preliminary
**1. Short title and commencement**  
This Act may be cited as the Clan and Family Sovereign Wealth Trusts Act, 2026, and shall come into operation upon publication in the Gazette, subject to reconciliation with the Sovereign Family and Clan Institutions Act, 2026.

**2. Interpretation**  
In this Act—  
“Clan Trust” means the primary multi-generational sovereign economic trust of a registered Clan.  
“Subsidiary Family Trust” means a sub-unit established for a nuclear/immediate family under the Clan Trust.  
“Patriarch” has the meaning in the Sovereign Family and Clan Institutions Act (No. 4).  
This Act shall be read together with Act No. 4 and subject to the Constitution. Provisions strengthening patriarchal governance are presented for parliamentary debate where they may require constitutional adjustment.

**3. Purpose**  
The purpose of this Act is to—  
(a) Create robust, Patriarch-led Clan and Family Sovereign Wealth Trusts as the economic backbone of the Sovereign Family Institution;  
(b) Mandate structured contributions, prudent stewardship, and generational wealth preservation to support pro-natalist family growth, welfare from conception to natural death, and reduction of dependence on the State;  
(c) Empower Patriarchs and Councils of Elders with strong governance authority while providing protections against external seizure and excessive taxation.

### Part II – Establishment and Governance
**4. Establishment of Clan Trust**  
(1) A Clan Trust is established upon the marriage of the founding Patriarch as a sovereign economic trust aligned with the Sovereign Family Institution.  
(2) Every Clan member (by blood, marriage, or adoption by oath) automatically holds an individual account within the Clan Trust.  
(3) Clans with twenty or more adult members shall support governance through a Council of Elders.  
(4) All Trusts must be registered with the County government and a national registry, submitting their trust deed outlining governance, contributions, and succession.

**5. Patriarchal Governance**  
(1) The Patriarch serves as the Executive Head of the Clan Trust with primary authority over strategic decisions, investments, distributions, and enforcement.  
(2) The Council of Elders advises the Patriarch and must approve major decisions (e.g., expenditures exceeding ten percent (10%) of Trust value) by two-thirds vote.  
(3) Required officers include the Treasury Elder and Records Elder, each supported by up to two Deacons.  
(4) The Patriarch and Council of Elders determine the balance between member distributions, Clan projects, reserves for generational prosperity, education, welfare, and pro-natalist support (e.g., marriage and child-rearing grants).

**6. Subsidiary Family Trusts**  
(1) Upon marriage of a Clan member, a Subsidiary Family Trust is automatically established under the Clan Trust.  
(2) The wife is adopted into the husband’s Clan and Family Trust, bringing her dowry as capital.  
(3) The Subsidiary Family Trust handles immediate nuclear family needs while remaining fully subject to overarching Clan Trust governance and Patriarchal authority.

### Part III – Contributions, Distributions and Operations
**7. Mandatory Contributions**  
(1) All working Clan members shall contribute one-fifth (1/5) of their gross income to the Clan Trust.  
(2) Contributions support collective welfare, investment, and pro-natalist objectives.  
(3) The Patriarch and Council may place liens or temporary restrictions on individual accounts for discipline, protection, welfare, or repayment of Clan support (proportionate and not exceeding seven years without court approval).

**8. Distributions and Profit Sharing**  
(1) At least twenty percent (20%) of all Clan Trust income shall be distributed equally among member accounts.  
(2) Each Subsidiary Family Trust shall receive fifty percent (50%) of the distribution share attributable to its members for immediate family needs.  
(3) The Patriarch and Council shall allocate the remainder to reserves, Clan projects, education funds, marriage incentives, child-rearing support, and welfare for vulnerable members (minors, elderly, widows).  
(4) Mandatory distributions and internal expenditures within the Trust shall enjoy preferential tax treatment.

### Part IV – Inheritance, Succession and Renewal
**9. Inheritance Upon Death**  
(1) **Family Trust Holdings**: Distributed equally among living children and the surviving spouse. Predeceased children’s shares pass per stirpes to their living children.  
(2) **Clan Trust Holdings**: Eighty percent (80%) follows the per stirpes principle; twenty percent (20%) is allocated at the discretion of the Patriarch and Council of Elders for collective benefit.  
(3) Similar rules apply to any Federal or State Sovereign Wealth Fund accounts held by members.  
(4) No automatic transfer of personal debts to heirs.

**10. Succession of Patriarch**  
(1) Upon the death of the Patriarch, the Clan Trust remains intact. A new Patriarch is selected within one year in accordance with Act No. 4.  
(2) In default, the Court may appoint an interim Elder as Patriarch, subject to Clan ratification.

**11. Clan Renewal and Division**  
Every fifth generation, or every one hundred (100) years (whichever occurs later), the Clan Trust shall undergo structured division into three to twelve new independent Clans. No successor Clan shall exceed one hundred families. Division is conducted by the Patriarch and Council with equitable asset allocation to preserve welfare and continuity.

### Part V – Incentives, Protections and Taxation
**12. Tax Reliefs and Pro-Natalist Incentives**  
(1) Registered Clan Trusts and enterprises they control shall claim tax relief of two percent (2%) for every order of magnitude of direct employees (capped at twenty percent (20%) of the total tax bill at Federal or County level).  
(2) Internal transfers, investments, and expenditures within Clan and Family Trusts are subject only to sales tax (exempt from income tax).  
(3) Enhanced child tax relief, education savings, marriage grants, and basic needs exemptions apply to support larger families and generational continuity.  
(4) Withdrawals to external personal accounts are subject to normal income tax.

**13. Strong Protection from Seizure**  
(1) Clan and Family Sovereign Wealth Trusts enjoy robust protection from government seizure or attachment.  
(2) In cases of individual fraud or misconduct, the State may claim only the proven direct proceeds from the offence. Innocent members and collective Trust assets are fully protected.  
(3) This protection reinforces the sovereign character of the Family Institution.

**14. Insurance, Welfare and Collective Powers**  
(1) Clan Trusts may maintain unified health, life, and welfare insurance for all members.  
(2) Clans may collectively bargain for housing, employment, and services.  
(3) The Clan holds collective responsibility for member welfare from conception to natural death.

### Part VI – Limits on Intervention and Miscellaneous
**15. Limits on State Intervention**  
The State or courts shall intervene in Clan Trust affairs only after exhaustion of internal Patriarchal and Elder processes and upon clear judicial finding of fraud, gross misconduct, systematic abuse, or obstruction of justice. Intervention shall be minimal and proportionate.

**16. Public Office and Reporting**  
Clans shall maintain accurate membership and Trust registries with County governments. Public office restrictions under Act No. 4 apply.

**17. Transition, Registration and Regulations**  
(1) Existing family wealth arrangements and cultural trusts may transition into this framework within thirty-six (36) months.  
(2) The Cabinet Secretary for Finance, in consultation with the Attorney-General and County governments, shall issue detailed regulations for registration, auditing, dispute resolution, and enforcement.  
(3) Parliament notes that strengthened mandatory contributions, Patriarchal control, and asset protections may require reconciliation with individual property and equality provisions in the Constitution.

**18. Savings and Supremacy**  
Documented alternative arrangements by consenting parties are respected, with this Act serving as the default framework. In all cases, the Constitution prevails unless amended.
