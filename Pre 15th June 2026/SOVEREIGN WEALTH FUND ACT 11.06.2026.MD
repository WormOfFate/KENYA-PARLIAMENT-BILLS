**The Sovereign Wealth Fund Act, 2026**

**A Bill for an Act of Parliament to establish a single Kenya Sovereign Wealth Fund as an instrument for intergenerational wealth creation, citizen stakeholding, economic resilience, and reduced fiscal dependence, consistent with Articles 10, 201, 203, 206, 209–212 and Chapter Twelve of the Constitution of Kenya, 2010.**

**ENACTED by the Parliament of Kenya as follows:**

### Part I – Preliminary
**1. Short title and commencement**  
This Act may be cited as the Sovereign Wealth Fund Act, 2026, and shall come into operation upon publication in the Gazette.

**2. Interpretation**  
In this Act—  
“Fund” means the Kenya Sovereign Wealth Fund established under this Act.  
“Account Holder” means a citizen or eligible resident with an account in the Fund.  
“Board” means the Board of Trustees of the Fund.  
This Act shall be read together with the Taxation and Fiscal Responsibility Act, 2026, the Family and Clan Sovereign Wealth Trusts Act, 2026, and the Public Finance Management Act, and is subject to the supremacy of the Constitution of Kenya, 2010.

**3. Purpose**  
The Kenya Sovereign Wealth Fund is the collective property of the people of Kenya. It shall build long-term national and generational wealth, promote intergenerational prosperity, reduce dependence on taxation and borrowing, and give every citizen and family a direct stake in the nation’s economic success.

### Part II – Establishment and Governance
**4. Establishment**  
(1) There is established a single public fund known as the Kenya Sovereign Wealth Fund, which shall operate as a limited liability holding company for investment and wealth preservation purposes.  
(2) The Fund is vested in the people of Kenya and managed independently of routine government operations.

**5. Board of Trustees**  
(1) The Fund shall be governed by a Board of twelve (12) Trustees, chaired by the President or a designated representative.  
(2) Trustees shall be natural-born Kenyan citizens with at least fourteen (14) years of private-sector experience in trade, industry, or finance.  
(3) Trustees shall serve non-renewable terms of fourteen (14) years, subject only to impeachment for malfeasance or conviction in a court of law.  
(4) A Trustee shall face summary suspension upon the opening of any criminal or civil proceedings. The Board and Fund accounts shall be frozen if four (4) or more Trustees are suspended, pending a full independent audit.

**6. Executive Management – Chief Executive Officer**  
(1) The Board shall vet candidates for Chief Executive Officer (CEO), who must be a Kenyan citizen with at least seven (7) years of private-sector experience.  
(2) Parliament (National Assembly) may review rejected applicants and propose up to two additional candidates.  
(3) Candidates shall undergo public hearings in the Senate. The Board shall present five to seven finalists to the Annual General Meeting of Account Holders for election by single transferable vote for a three-year term (renewable up to a maximum of three terms).  
(4) The CEO shall exercise plenary powers for day-to-day operations and execution of Board-approved policy, subject to weekly reporting to the Board and annual audits.  
(5) The CEO may be removed by a two-thirds (⅔) no-confidence vote of the Board or Account Holders.  
(6) Performance bonuses shall be determined by the Board, approved by Account Holders, held in escrow, and paid only seven years after leaving office following successful audit.

**7. Deputies**  
The CEO may appoint up to twenty-one (21) Deputies with limited executive powers. Deputies must be qualified for CEO duties, confirmed by the Board, and may not serve consecutive terms under the same CEO. The CEO may dismiss Deputies without cause, subject to Board review.

### Part III – Capitalization, Income and Operations
**8. Initial Capitalization Phase (First Seven Years)**  
(1) For the first seven years, eighty percent (80%) of net revenues, investment returns, and profits shall be retained for capitalization and growth.  
(2) Twenty percent (20%) shall be distributed to Account Holders. Withdrawals are restricted in the first three years.  
(3) The Fund automatically transitions to full operations after seven years unless Parliament extends the phase by a two-thirds (⅔) vote for up to two additional years.

**9. Sources of Income**  
The Fund shall be capitalized through:  
(a) At least ten percent (10%) of all national revenue collections (as provided in the Taxation and Fiscal Responsibility Act);  
(b) Profits and capital appreciation from its investments and assets;  
(c) Voluntary deposits by citizens, Clans, Families, and communities.

### Part IV – Account Holders, Distributions and Withdrawals
**10. Account Holders**  
(1) Every Kenyan citizen shall automatically receive an account upon birth or legal recognition. Eligible residents receive accounts upon legal entry, with full rights upon naturalization.  
(2) Only citizens have full voting, investment, and withdrawal rights. Accounts continue for life.  
(3) Upon death, holdings are distributed equally among living children or their heirs (per stirpes).  
(4) Account Holders receive quarterly financial statements.

**11. Distributions of Profits (Post-Initial Phase)**  
(1) Eighty percent (80%) of net profits (after operating costs) shall be shared equally among all Account Holders.  
(2) Twenty percent (20%) shall be retained as reserves.  
(3) Every seventh year, a special major equal distribution of accumulated surplus shall be made, retaining only twenty percent (20%) in reserves.  
(4) Account Holders may vote (70% quorum) to approve higher retention for up to three successive years.

**12. Withdrawals and Incentives**  
(1) After the initial phase, Account Holders may withdraw up to ten percent (10%) of their balance per month. Parliament may temporarily raise this to thirty percent (30%) by two-thirds vote for one year.  
(2) Citizens may invest or lend through the Fund’s internal marketplace, retaining eighty percent (80%) of profits.  
(3) Up to twenty percent (20%) of an account may be used as collateral for approved activities.  
(4) Special large withdrawals of up to eighty percent (80%) are permitted upon citizenship milestones or every six years.  
(5) Monthly vouchers equivalent to three percent (3%) of holdings (valid for 12 months) may be issued.  
(6) Citizens may grant limited gratuities from their accounts to Executive Officers (subject to strict caps and equal departmental distribution), taxed as normal income.

### Part V – Investments, Taxation Status and Safeguards
**13. Investment and Credit Roles**  
(1) The Fund shall invest in strategic industries, provide citizen credit pools, and facilitate regulated peer-to-peer brokerage.  
(2) No more than thirty percent (30%) of holdings shall be in any single industry.  
(3) The Fund is strictly prohibited from usury and creating systemic national debt.

**14. Taxation Status**  
(1) The Fund itself shall not pay taxes.  
(2) Internal dividends, returns, and profits credited to accounts are tax-exempt.  
(3) External withdrawals are subject to normal income tax.  
(4) Internal expenditures and investments are subject only to sales tax.

**15. Governance and Accountability**  
(1) The Board shall present quarterly audited reports and hold Annual General Meetings.  
(2) Trustees and the CEO bear personal liability for gross negligence or corruption.  
(3) The Fund has no legislative or enforcement powers.  
(4) Citizens and registered communities may make deposits with an 80/20 split mechanism benefiting targeted and general accounts.

### Part VI – County Integration and Miscellaneous
**16. County Integration**  
County Governments may establish subsidiary wealth mechanisms or participate through Clan and Family Trusts that link with the national Fund. KRA shall audit all related processes in accordance with the Taxation and Fiscal Responsibility Act.

**17. Transition**  
The Fund shall be seeded through a combination of revenue allocations, asset transfers, and appropriations as approved by Parliament. Existing sovereign wealth arrangements shall be integrated within twenty-four (24) months.

**18. Regulations**  
The Cabinet Secretary for Finance, in consultation with the Board, Council of Governors, and Auditor-General, shall issue regulations for account management, audits, citizen participation, and operations.

**19. Constitutional Note and Savings**  
Certain provisions (long Board terms, citizen voting in CEO selection, mandatory revenue allocation, and strong independence) are presented for parliamentary debate and possible constitutional amendment. In case of inconsistency, the Constitution prevails unless amended.
