# **The Direct Citizen Benefit and Public Service Reform Act, 2026**

**A Bill for an Act of Parliament to shift public spending from institutional bureaucracy to direct end-user support in education, healthcare, and agriculture, while integrating and progressively overriding the Social Health Authority (SHA) and the New University Funding Model through a structured 10%-100% phasing mechanism, consistent with Articles 43, 53, 201, 210–212 and Chapters Four and Twelve of the Constitution of Kenya, 2010.**

**ENACTED by the Parliament of Kenya as follows:**

### Part I – Preliminary
**1. Short title and commencement**  
This Act may be cited as the Direct Citizen Benefit and Public Service Reform Act, 2026, and shall come into operation on such date as the Cabinet Secretary for Finance may appoint.

**2. Interpretation**  
“End-User Benefit” means direct stipends, vouchers, or price guarantees to citizens/households.  
“Phasing Percentage” means the gradual shift from institutional funding to direct end-user funding, starting at 10% in Year 1 and reaching 100% by Year 10.  
“SHA” means the Social Health Authority established under the Social Health Insurance Act, 2023.  
This Act shall be read together with prior Acts in this legislative package and is subject to the Constitution.

**3. Purpose**  
The purpose is to:  
(a) Implement direct end-user funding as the core philosophy;  
(b) Progressively incorporate and override inefficiencies in SHA and the New University Funding Model via a **10%-100% phasing model** over 10 years;  
(c) Retain state oversight on public institutions with enforced low profit margins and mandatory reinvestment.

### Part II – Phasing Mechanism (10%-100% Model)
**4. General Phasing Rules**  
(1) All reforms under this Act shall follow a **10%-100% phasing model**:  
   - Year 1: 10% of applicable budget shifted to direct end-user benefits.  
   - Subsequent years: Increase by 10% annually until 100% in Year 10.  
   - Institutional block funding (TSC, SHA capitation, university capitation, etc.) shall be reduced proportionally each year.  

(2) Savings from reduced bureaucracy shall be redirected to the Sovereign Wealth Fund and enhanced stipends.  
(3) Annual reports on phasing progress, outcomes, and adjustments shall be submitted to Parliament.

### Part III – Healthcare Reform (Incorporating/Overriding SHA)
**5. Minimum Hospital Stipends and SHA Integration**  
(1) Direct minimum hospital stipends shall be provided to all citizens for verified services at approved facilities.  
(2) **Phasing with SHA**:  
   - The stipends shall progressively replace SHA’s contributory and institutional model.  
   - In Year 1 (10%), stipends cover 10% of eligible services; SHA continues for the remainder.  
   - By Year 10 (100%), direct stipends fully replace SHA institutional funding. Citizens retain optional supplementary private insurance.  
   - SHA contributions and operations shall be wound down proportionally; unutilised funds transferred to the Sovereign Wealth Fund.  

(3) Public hospitals remain under state oversight with capped profit margins and mandatory reinvestment of surpluses.  
(4) Stipends shall be disbursed digitally (e.g., via Sovereign Wealth Fund accounts or mobile wallets) upon service verification to minimise fraud.

**6. Accountability in Healthcare**  
Facilities must publish service and outcome data. Payments are tied to verified patient outcomes and quality standards. Retroactive audits apply.

### Part IV – Education Reform (Incorporating/Overriding University Funding Model)
**7. School Fee Stipends (Basic and Secondary)**  
Direct school fee stipends to students/parents/guardians, phased 10%-100% as per Section 4, replacing TSC and institutional capitation. Public schools under state oversight with low margins and reinvestment mandates.

**8. University and Tertiary Funding Model Override**  
(1) The existing New University Funding Model (student-centred with scholarships, loans, and household contributions based on need) shall be progressively integrated and overridden.  
(2) **Phasing Approach**:  
   - Year 1 (10%): 10% of university funding shifted to direct student stipends/vouchers.  
   - Subsequent years: +10% annually until 100%.  
   - Direct stipends shall cover tuition, materials, and living allowances based on verified enrolment and performance.  
   - Institutional capitation to universities shall be reduced proportionally.  

(3) Public universities and TVET institutions shall operate under state oversight with capped administrative margins and mandatory reinvestment in research, infrastructure, and teaching quality.  
(4) Performance-based funding: Stipends and residual institutional support tied to graduation rates, employability, and innovation metrics.

### Part V – Agriculture Reform
**9. Minimum Sale Price Guarantee**  
As previously detailed, with direct benefits to farmers. Public agricultural institutions under oversight with low margins and reinvestment.

### Part VI – Oversight, Transition and Savings
**10. State Oversight of Public Institutions**  
All public schools, hospitals, and agricultural institutions remain under regulatory oversight to enforce quality, equity, and low profit margins. Surpluses must be reinvested in core services.

**11. Transition Safeguards**  
(1) A 10-year phased implementation plan with annual milestones and independent evaluation.  
(2) Protections for vulnerable groups during transition.  
(3) Savings redirected to Sovereign Wealth Fund citizen accounts and enhanced benefits.

**12. Regulations**  
Cabinet Secretaries for Finance, Education, Health, and Agriculture shall issue detailed regulations, including stipend formulas, MSP calculations, digital systems, and SHA/university transition protocols.

**13. Constitutional Note**  
This Act advances direct citizen benefits while progressively reforming SHA and the university funding model. The 10%-100% phasing ensures fiscal prudence and service continuity. Inconsistencies with existing laws are resolved in favour of this Act’s objectives, subject to constitutional supremacy.
