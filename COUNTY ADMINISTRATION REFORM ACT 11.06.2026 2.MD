# **The County Legislative and Council Act, 2026**

**A Bill for an Act of Parliament to establish a bi-cameral County Council system as the legislative and oversight arm of County Governments, building upon the County Governance and Executive Separation Act, 2026, and other related legislation, consistent with Articles 176, 179–187, 196–200 and Chapter Eleven of the Constitution of Kenya, 2010.**

**ENACTED by the Parliament of Kenya as follows:**

### Part I – Preliminary
**1. Short title and commencement**  
This Act may be cited as the County Legislative and Council Act, 2026, and shall come into operation upon publication in the Gazette, subject to any necessary constitutional amendments.

**2. Interpretation**  
In this Act—  
“Lower House” means the House of Elected Councillors.  
“Alderman Board” means the Upper House of the County Council.  
“County Council” means the bi-cameral legislative and oversight body of the County.  
This Act shall be read together with the County Governance and Executive Separation Act, 2026, the Taxation and Fiscal Responsibility Act, 2026, the Sovereign Wealth Fund Act, 2026, and other related laws, and is subject to the supremacy of the Constitution of Kenya, 2010.

**3. Purpose**  
The purpose of this Act is to—  
(a) Establish a bi-cameral County Council to enhance deliberative democracy, citizen participation through random draft, legislative oversight, and coordination between civil administration and law enforcement;  
(b) Provide for local legislation, budget oversight, and accountability mechanisms that support security, prosperity, and welfare at the County level.

### Part II – Establishment of the Bi-Cameral County Council
**4. Composition**  
(1) Each County shall have a bi-cameral County Council consisting of:  
   (a) A Lower House of Elected Councillors; and  
   (b) An Upper House known as the Alderman Board.  

(2) The County Council shall handle local governance, infrastructure policy, budget administration, support for law enforcement and civil administration, and other matters assigned by law or County Charter.

**5. Officers of Each House**  
Each House shall elect from its membership a Speaker, Secretary, and Treasurer to manage proceedings, records, and internal accountability.

### Part III – The Lower House (House of Elected Councillors)
**6. Composition and Term**  
(1) The Lower House shall consist of an even number of ward representatives, each serving a two (2) year term, renewable up to a maximum of five (5) terms.  
(2) Each Ward shall elect an even number of representatives at the ratio of one (1) per one hundred thousand (100,000) residents, with a minimum of two (2) per ward.  
(3) A County Council Chair and Deputy Chair shall be elected county-wide for a six (6) year term.

**7. Qualifications**  
A person qualifies for election to the Lower House if they—  
(a) are at least twenty-four (24) years of age;  
(b) have no criminal conviction or incarceration within the prior seven (7) years.

**8. Non-Voting Members**  
The following shall be non-voting members of the Lower House:  
(a) The County Sheriff and County Attorney, who shall attend to give account of their conduct;  
(b) County Representatives in the National Assembly and Senate, who shall attend at least one session per month.  
The County Council may impeach a National Assembly or Senate representative by a two-thirds (⅔) vote, subject to confirmation by the Alderman Board. The impeached representative may appeal to the Senate.

**9. Powers of the County Council Chair**  
The Chair shall—  
(a) serve as the County’s representative in the relevant national legislative body where applicable;  
(b) exercise a final veto on County by-laws or resolutions;  
(c) serve as a sitting member of all County commissions.

### Part IV – The Upper House (Alderman Board)
**10. Composition and Term**  
(1) The Alderman Board shall consist of members drafted by public physical random lot from a county-wide pool of valid electors who participated in the immediate previous County elections and have been resident in the County for at least two previous election cycles.  
(2) The number of Aldermen shall be twice the membership of the Lower House.  
(3) Members serve one-year terms, staggered every four (4) months (one-third drafted every four months).  
(4) A person may not serve a new term within three (3) years of completing a prior term or holding any other legislative or executive office.

**11. Role and Proceedings**  
(1) The Alderman Board shall participate in debates and deliberations of the County Council but shall vote only as the Upper House.  
(2) The Board shall hold separate sessions on a different day later in the same week as the Lower House.

**12. Powers and Functions of the Alderman Board**  
(1) **Legislative Review**: Review, amend, approve, or reject all legislation passed by the Lower House. No County law shall take effect without approval by both Houses.  
(2) **Advisory Interpretation**: Issue written opinions on legislation and governance for guidance to courts and future councils.  
(3) **Oversight and Inquiry**: Investigate public concerns, administrative misconduct, or misuse of authority; summon documents and request testimony.  
(4) **Consent on Extraordinary Measures**: Approve or reject emergency appropriations, suspension of procedures, or major reforms.  
(5) **Arbitration**: Aldermen may serve as arbitrators in rental, license, or civil disputes. Affected parties may petition for binding arbitration (subject to formal court appeal). Petitions shall be randomly allocated if not voluntarily accepted.

### Part V – Responsibilities of the County Council
**13. General Responsibilities**  
The County Council shall—  
(a) Regulate local affairs, including those assigned by the Constitution, national law, or County Charter;  
(b) Coordinate between the Mayor (civil administration) and the County Sheriff (law enforcement);  
(c) Appoint and oversee the County Revenue Commissioner;  
(d) Make responsive legislative decisions and appropriations guided by Specific, Measurable, Attainable, Realistic, and Timely (SMART) goals that improve security, prosperity, and welfare of residents.

### Part VI – Miscellaneous
**14. Transition and Integration**  
(1) Existing County Assemblies shall transition to this bi-cameral structure within thirty-six (36) months, in coordination with the County Governance and Executive Separation Act, 2026.  
(2) The County Governor shall facilitate the appointment of the Mayor, while elections and drafting for the Council shall be managed by the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) in consultation with the County.

**15. Regulations**  
The Cabinet Secretary responsible for devolution, in consultation with the Council of Governors and IEBC, shall issue regulations for elections, random drafting, impeachment procedures, and operations of the bi-cameral Council.

**16. Constitutional Note and Savings**  
This Act builds upon the separated executive framework (Sheriff, Mayor appointed by County Governor, Revenue Commissioner) established in the County Governance and Executive Separation Act, 2026. Provisions on bi-cameralism, random draft of Aldermen, County Chair veto, and impeachment of national representatives may require constitutional amendment for full implementation. In case of any inconsistency, the Constitution of Kenya, 2010, prevails unless amended.

This bill completes the County-level governance framework in the successive legislative series. It integrates directly with the previous County Executive Separation Act (where the Mayor is appointed by the County Governor and the Cabinet consists of MCAs) and maintains coherence with the broader package (Judiciary, Family/Clan Trusts, Taxation, Sovereign Wealth Fund).
